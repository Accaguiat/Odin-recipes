This page is all about menudo.

Menudo is another cuisine originated from the philippines.

Menudo in the Philippines is a popular, savory stew that differs significantly from its Mexican counterpart. Filipino menudo is made with diced pork (or sometimes chicken), liver, potatoes, carrots, bell peppers, and tomatoes. It's cooked in a tomato-based sauce, often seasoned with soy sauce, garlic, onions, and a variety of spices. The dish is commonly served with rice.

Recipe of menudo

  • 1 lb (450g) pork shoulder or belly, diced into small cubes
  • 1/4 lb (115g) pork liver, diced into small cubes (optional)
  • 2 medium potatoes, peeled and diced
  • 1 large carrot, peeled and diced
  • 1/2 cup green peas (optional)
  • 1 small red bell pepper, diced
  • 1 small green bell pepper, diced
  • 1 medium onion, chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 medium tomatoes, chopped
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup tomato sauce
  • 2 cups water or chicken broth
  • 1-2 tbsp cooking oil
  • 1-2 bay leaves
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 tbsp sugar (optional)
  • 1/2 tsp paprika or annatto powder (optional, for color)
  • 1 tbsp raisins (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Marinate the Pork:
    • In a bowl, combine the diced pork with soy sauce. Marinate for at least 30 minutes to an hour for better flavor.
  2. ,Sautéing:
    • Heat the cooking oil in a large pot or deep pan over medium heat.
    • Add the chopped onions and garlic. Sauté until the onions become translucent and the garlic is fragrant.
    • Add the chopped tomatoes and cook until they soften and break down into a sauce.
  3. Cooking the Pork:
    • Add the marinated pork to the pot. Cook until the meat is browned on all sides.
    • If using pork liver, add it now and cook for a few minutes until it's slightly browned.
  4. Simmering:
    • Pour in the tomato sauce and water or chicken broth. Add the bay leaves.
    • Bring to a boil, then lower the heat and let it simmer for about 20-30 minutes, or until the pork is tender.
  5. Adding Vegetables:
    • Add the diced potatoes and carrots to the pot. Cook until they are tender, about 10-15 minutes.
    • Stir in the bell peppers, green peas, and raisins (if using). Continue to cook for another 5 minutes.
  6. Final Touches:
    • Season with salt, pepper, and sugar (if desired). Add paprika or annatto powder for color, if using.
    • Let the menudo simmer for a few more minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together.
  7. Serve:
    • Remove the bay leaves before serving.
    • Serve the menudo hot with steamed rice.

Enjoy your homemade Filipino menudo!
